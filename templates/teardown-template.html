01 TL;DR

The gap looks small. It isn't.

Two percentage points between Brand A and Brand B. The headline gap hides three structurally different competitive patterns, each with its own remedy.

The structural pattern

Brand A has dedicated /solutions/ product pages for every core topic — workation, business travel, A1 certificates, EU posted-worker directive, approval flows, risk assessment. The pages exist and are indexed at Peec as PRODUCT_PAGE.

The problem isn't missing content — it's inconsistent AI discoverability: some of these pages get retrieved and cited reliably, others never.

The A1 gap is a discovery problem

On Prompts 3 (Workation) and 5 (Approval) the system works as intended: the relevant /solutions/ pages get retrieved and cited.

On Prompt 4 (A1), the existing page /solutions/features/a1-certificates-cocs appears in none of the seven analyzed chats. AI engines retrieve Brand A wiki articles instead. The page exists; it isn't being found.

Fact-check · the Brand B liability claim

One of the most striking statements in the entire analysis. Perplexity claimed Brand B promises "liability through the system." If true, it would be a commercial differentiator. We checked.

RESOLVED
AI engines amplify marketing language into legal claims
The claim: Brand B promises "automated generation and liability through the system".
Source: Perplexity, Apr 21, 2026, prompt pr_be79e238.
Source check
The Brand B A1 solutions page (brand-b.com/en/solutions/a1-certificates) was fully retrieved and reviewed via get_url_content.
What the page says
The page uses phrases like "protect your company and your employees from risk", "100% legally compliant business travel", "global coverage", "fulfill your duty of care".
What the page does NOT say
No explicit claim that the system assumes legal liability. No phrasing that promises a transfer of legal liability to Brand B. The protection language refers to compliance support, not liability transfer.
Verdict
⚠ UNVERIFIED · Perplexity amplification

Perplexity aggregated marketing language ("protection", "100% compliant", "global coverage") into a stronger legal claim ("liability through the system") than the source page actually makes. This is a generic AI artifact, not Brand B's communication.
Strategic implication
For Brand A: Sales can counter with facts. Brand B itself doesn't claim liability assumption. Methodologically: Stage 5 (fact-check) is not optional. Without it, a naive analysis would accept Perplexity's aggregation as competitive intelligence.
RESOLVED · CORRECTION
The Brand A A1 page exists and is indexed
Initial hypothesis: Brand A has no dedicated A1 product page, only wiki articles. That's why Brand B is preferred for citation.
Flagged by analyst feedback: The page brand-a.com/solutions/features/a1-certificates-cocs does in fact exist.
Verification
get_url_content on brand-a.com/solutions/features/a1-certificates-cocs. Peec has indexed the page, last scrape Apr 16, 2026. Classification: CORPORATE / PRODUCT_PAGE.
Content quality
Strong product page with quantified claims: 15,732 certificates issued, avg. 30 min time saved per certificate, avg. 35% cost reduction. Clear CTAs, feature structure, interlinking to wiki content.
Retrieval status
Despite being indexed, this URL appears in none of the 7 analyzed A1 chats in the retrieval pool. Brand A wiki articles are retrieved instead. This is a discovery problem, not a content problem.
Corrected verdict
✓ PAGE EXISTS · DISCOVERY FAILURE

The gap doesn't close by building a new page — it closes by diagnosing the existing one: URL depth, H1 phrasing, schema, internal linking, backlinks. Possible drivers of non-discoverability are listed in action item 01.
Methodological lesson
The Peec MCP workflow makes it possible to distinguish between not indexed, indexed but not retrieved, and retrieved but not cited — three fundamentally different diagnoses with three different fixes. Without that granularity, the analysis would have recommended the wrong action ("build the page") instead of the right one ("make the page discoverable").

Action plan · Prompt 04

01
On-page · Discovery

Discovery audit of the existing A1 page

The page brand-a.com/solutions/features/a1-certificates-cocs exists and is indexed at Peec as PRODUCT_PAGE, but is not retrieved in any of the analyzed A1 chats. Five concrete checks: (1) Query-to-heading alignment — current H1 "A1 Certificates & CoCs" vs. Brand B "Apply for A1 certificates fully automatically". (2) URL depth — 3 levels vs. Brand B's 2; consider an additional short URL /solutions/a1-certificates with redirect. (3) Schema.org Product/SoftwareApplication markup. (4) Internal link density from wiki pages to the solutions page. (5) External backlinks with query-relevant anchor text.

02
On-page · Content

Wiki-to-solution link audit

The three wiki pages on the A1 topic are retrieved by Google AI Overview (and occasionally cited), but don't prominently link back to the solutions page. Result: AI sees wiki content, treats it as educational material, and offers no strong path to the product page. Fix: every A1 wiki page gets a contextual link to the solutions page in the first third of the article, with clear anchor text ("A1 certificate software" instead of "Learn more").

03
Editorial

Outreach to triplolo.com, relokatehr.com, and clevis.de

Independently confirmed by Peec. Peec Actions (scope=editorial, LISTICLE) lists triplolo.com/10-best-corporate-travel-management-platforms-for-2026 as a high-opportunity target. Pitch with specific positioning: Germany-first, social-security filing portal integration, HR system import.

04
Editorial

Perplexity discovery audit

On Apr 22, Brand A wasn't even in Perplexity's retrieval pool. That isn't a ranking problem — it's a discovery problem. Check: sitemap coverage, canonical setup, English vs. German URL variant, robots.txt accessibility for Perplexity's crawler.

05
Sales enablement

Battlecard · Brand B liability claim · fact-check resolved

The fact-check (above) resolved: Brand B itself does not claim liability assumption on its own product page. Perplexity amplified marketing language. Sales can now counter with facts: "According to Brand B's own product page, Brand B does not claim liability assumption. The statement cited by AI is an extrapolation, not a vendor claim."
